No Man's Sky Review

  • by:L. Arbuckle
  • date:23 September, 2019
  • 3 Comments

No Man's Sky, a game by Hello Games, is about to have a huge update - the Beyond update. Last week, I purchased No Man's Sky on Steam and I was very lucky to get it on sale. I've sinked 8 hours into it so far, and I'm eager to share my thoughts about it with you.

No Man's Sky is an action-adventure survival game developed and published by the indie studio Hello Games. The game is built around four pillars: exploration, trading, construction and combat

The game is built around 4 pillars: exploration, trading, construction, and combat.

How did I hear about No Man's Sky?

I was watching Youtube one day when I came across a video titled "A game with over a trillion planets". I wasn't really aware of clickbait at the time, and I'm glad I wasn't, because the title wasn't clickbait (as of right now, there are over 18 Quintillion planets in NMS). So I watched the video, and the early footage looked really good. I wasn't really sure about it, however, as the video discussed the combat elements, and at the time I wasn't really into games mostly about combat (which was what I assumed it to be about at the time). Today, I don't mind blasting the odd alien NPC, but I prefer (like I would in real life) cooperating with the aliens and learning from them, which is of course what you can do (scientists at the science terminals are an example, which I will dicuss later.).

What was the first thing I did in NMS?

I carefully read what each gamemode (permadeath, creative, normal, survival) meant before jumping into the game, and I then jumped into a Normal world type and started following the tutorial. The tutorial mission really helps new players (like myself) get acquantinced with the game and the storyline, which I'm still following, is almost the best I've ever seen.
