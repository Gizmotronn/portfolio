Was Benzema a better player at Lyon than Lacazette?

by Liam Arbuckle

Both Bezema and Lacazette were truly giants for Lyon. They both have a close average of goals per game, with Lacazette ahead slightly on 0.46 goals per game compared to Benzema’s 0.45 per game. If you look away from the stats for a minute and look only at the team success, which is what matters most in football, Benzema is a better player: 4 Ligue 1 titles with Lyon (from the 2004/5 season to the 2007/8 season. Lyon won a record 7 straight titles, their only in the current iteration of the top flight of French football) 2 Trophee des champions (in 2006/7 and 2007/8) Coupe de France (2007/8) Benzema won 4 league titles and 3 domestic cups with Lyon, which is a phenomenal record considering he was only at Lyon from 2004–2009 before departing for Spanish giants Real Madrid, where he won a further set of league titles and the one trophy that eluded him at Lyon - the Champions League. However, the quality of a player is not dependent on the success of the team (one only has to look at Messi in the international tournaments. If you saw his record of no international trophies, you could assume that he is not a good international player. But that is far from the truth. He is an absolute legend that just had the misfortune to play for a dismal Argentine side). Lacazette had no team success with Lyon, but had an average of 0.45 goals per game our of 267 games for Lyon. He has a smattering of individual awards he has won with Lyon, like Benzema. However, Benzema at Lyon was better than Lacazette. While team success isn’t everything, Benzema won 7 domestic titles, had a higher goal and assist average and more individual awards, which are a good way of determining the value of a player. Lacazette recently moved to Arsenal and, unless he moves back to Lyon was not a better player than Benzema (and if so, why didn’t Real come calling?).
