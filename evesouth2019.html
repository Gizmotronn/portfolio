Southampton vs Everton 2019/20

  • By Liam
  • Nov 9th 2019

A goal from Danny Ings wasn't enough for the Saints as they suffered their second successive home loss, this time to Everton. Goals to Tom Davies (4') and Richarlison (74') lifted some pressure off of manager Marco Silva, and injected some much-needed confidence into the side going into the international break.

As an Everton fan, I know all too well that the international break isn't usually a very happy time for us Evertonians, so isn't it nice and refreshing to experience a weekend without football without feeling disappointed?

It wasn't all plain sailing. For about 30 minutes, the scores were level. The majority of that was after we'd (yet again) conceded from a set piece (this is debatable, considering how long it was between the corner and Ing's goal). Those 24 minutes or so were horribly painful for us Evertonians, especially because there were a number of chances that Southampton almost took (including the free kick) and we weren't playing the same way we were in the first half.

In that first half, we dominated, with around 18 shots. Southampton looked helpless, they had no attempts on goal until after the half-time break. We dominated possession and, although it was against Southampton (who had lost 9-0 at home to Leicster a few weeks before), there were encouraging signs.

Losing Bernard when we played Brighton was a big loss.

Despite all the encouraging signs from the first half, we should have put the game to be in the first half. With the domination that we had been displaying in the first half, we could have easily gone up by 2 or 3 goals before half-time, which would have likely put the Saints players under more pressure, leaving them less likely to score against Pickford (who, in his years in the top flight of English Football, has still not managed to keep a clean sheet against Southampton). This lack of finishing ability is something that the fans have been talking about for months, and something I'll go over in a moment.

We went into the half-time break feeling good. We weren't too tired, we were dominating both in the stats department and physically as well. Surely we were set for a big second half, we all thought. The ease at which we had kicked the football around in that first half meant that anything less than the same would not be acceptable for the fans or the manager.

The manager for Southampton made a change, bringing Sofiane Boufal on and taking Redmond off. Redmond had found the ball frequently in the first half, but the manager chose to inject some pace into the line-up for the second half.

It was this change that ultimately undid all the good work we'd done in that first half. Boufal created a chance from a corner in the 50th minute and did a fast cutback that ended up in the path of Danny Ings. He took the opportunity and the scores were level.
