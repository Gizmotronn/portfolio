WAAIFL Round 12 2019 - Willetton vs High Wycombe Review

Willetton defeated High Wycombe at Steel Blue Oval on August 3, 2019, 85-27.

I drove into Steel Blue Oval with Ash on Saturday morning. I had to get up at 5.30 in the morning to be able to get to the ground on time, as we had to be at Steel Blue at 8 am for the warm-up and a 9 am start. I trained with Trent and Ash, mainly doing some goalkicking drills. The rest of the team arrived at about 7.45 am, and we went into the rooms to get our jerseys on. I had my shoulder and wrist strapped up shortly before running out with the boys as it had been whacked by Trent during the warmup.

Willetton came into the match against High Wycombe needing to win to consolidate their top 4 spot, while High Wycombe had come in off a stirring victory against Wembley. Willetton had lost their previous game, against Kingsway, while High Wycombe had won 4 of their past 5. Before their loss to Kingsway, Willetton had won 6 games straight, including victories against Coolbinia (1st) and Warnbro (5th).

Bounce down

Willetton won the first clearance but elected to go backwards, and a sloppy kick from Fleming allowed Graham Govun (High Wycombe #30) to take a mark about 30 meters out from their attacking 50. "Guv" chose to handpass, and Arbuckle intercepted and kicked towards Schulz, who marked and sent Willetton inside 50 for the first time.

Arbuckle scored the first goal for Willetton, a nice shot on the run, however as I prepared to kick it I raised my right arm too far and the strapping on my shoulder ripped off, meaning my shoulder popped out again. I had to get off the ground as soon as I had kicked the goal and since we had no interchange (Matthew & John had been omitted from the side) we were a man down for about a minute as I had my shoulder re-strapped.

The six points:

  • Liam Arbuckle is currently on 24 goals for the season. He has scored a goal in only 1 loss - against High Wycombe. In that game, he scored 3
  • Willetton will be welcoming back 4 state players, while High Wycombe welcome back 2 state players. The line-ups of both teams will change because of this - who will be more versatile?
  • If Willetton win, it will be their first win against High Wycombe in league history (they did beat them in a curtain raiser before a WAFL game in 2017, though)
  • Willetton have lost 3 matches this year, including one last week. In the matches immediately after their first two losses, Arbuckle scored 4 goals both times (against Wembley & Warnbro)
  • Willetton and High Wycombe play very different styles of footy - Willetton play with the run-and-carry style of the great Essendon and Geelong teams, while High Wycombe rely on brute strength to get the ball and bomb it into the forward line.
  • Willetton have never lost at Steel Blue oval after 3 games there

It's a bit week for:

The Willetton Forwards - High Wycombe are very good at clearing the ball out of defense and keeping the ball in the midfield. If Willetton's forwards don't fire, it's hard to see them winning the match.

Big Calls

  • Marshall (Willetton coach) rolls the dice and throws Arbuckle forward, with the maestro scoring 4 goals and leading the Blues to victory
  • Willetton put their best players down back and play a form of park-the-bus after winning the first quarter

Prediction: High Wycombe by 19 points
