Kingsway vs Willetton Review

Home WAAIFL 2019 Qualifying Final

Willetton outlast Kingsway at the death

  • Sports, Football, Willetton
  • 10 Comments

In one of the matches of the year, Willetton outlasted Kingsway by 3 goals in the first qualifying final. Willetton will now play Coolbinia for a spot in the Grand Final, while Kingsway will brush themselves off as they play High Wycombe in the second week of the finals

Akor scored the first goal for Willetton, after a courageous diving mark and snap from over 35 meters out, however Kingsway, led by Crawford, pulled one back soon after and the first quarter finished with 1.4 to 1.0 in favour of Willetton.

Willetton conceded 3 goals to 1 in the second term. When Crawford marked in the square and goaled for Kingsway, the difference was a game-high 16 points. Willetton looked a shadow of the team that had thrashed High Wycombe just 2 weeks ago. Kingsway were playing like a team possessed, with silky passes almost leading to Willetton's defeat in the second quarter. If Kingsway had of been able to get their last set of touches just right (as in the passes inside 50 and the shots), Willetton would have been on their knees early. It was only thanks to veteran defender Schulz that so many of their inside-50 attempts were spoilt. Willetton went into the half-time break needing something to change. And something did.

Both teams trudged off to their rooms. Willetton's coach, Keilan Marshall, congratulated his team on their crisp passing, but told them to apply more pressure. He said to them to "start shepherding and protect your teammates. That's what I want to see from you boys this quarter".

Kingsway could feel it was a different team coming out of the away rooms in the second half. Willetton's intensity was renewed, and they slammed on 3 straight goals in the first 10 minutes to take a hard-fought 14 point lead. But still Kingsway wouldn't die, and a 50 metre penalty against Stewart, the captain for Willetton and a free kick for dangerous tackle, awarded to Crawford for Kingsway, tied the scores. Then Crawford marked 50 meters out in a paddock of space, and ran within 30 meters before slotting his second and putting his team a goal up at 3-quarter-time. Straight after this, Brad for Willetton limped off with a hamstring injury and put his tracksuit on. He was done for the day.

The game had been starting to run away from Willetton, and Stewart rallied his troops, telling them to

Play hard guys! Remember, if we win this we are one game away from the grand final. Do you see Brad? Do you see how much he wants to be out there with us? You're playing for him as well. We've lifted the intensity boys, we've done our blocks, our shepherds. We need to help out Gaz [Garry Schulz] & Kev (Kevin Phillips) in defense - they've been running all day and we haven't been getting back fast enough. If you're a midfielder, hang a kick behind the ball. The way to win this game is to play defensively

And they did. Willetton scrambled it forward through Arbuckle & Gould, but Arbuckle was tackled and a ball-up ensued. The ball stayed in the forward 50 meters for Willetton, with their tackling pressure so intense that any Kingsway kick out of there was marked by the dynamic wall and was kicked back in. But again, as it's been a lot of the season, Willetton couldn't find a target inside the 50.

With 4 minutes remaining, the ball was gathered by Schulz about 70 meters out and he slammed it onto his boot, looking for Gould for Willetton. Gould dropped the mark and the ball bobbled towards the behind post. Arbuckle and Crawford, the 2 vice-captains from either team, rushed for the ball, and Arbuckle dived on the ball, forcing it out of bounds and away from Crawford's searching hands.

The ball was thrown in by the boundary umpire. Zac Aquino tapped it down to Arbuckle, who was running into the contest. He snapped from about a meter from the behind post, and to his delight the ball went in.

The final bounce of the game ended with a free kick to Arbuckle, who had pushed into the middle for the bounce before being bumped off the ball by Wallington for Kingsway. With the ensuing free kick, Willetton milked the clock and the ball ended up in Stewart's hands just as the siren sounded.

What's next?

Kingsway play High Wycombe in the first Semi-Final after High Wycombe disposed of Warnbro in the elimination final. If Kingsway win, they go through to the Prelim final.

For Willetton, they play Coolbinia next week in the semi-final at Steel Blue Oval. Win, and they go straight through to the grand final. Whoever loses out of the blues and the falcons will play in the Prelim for a second shot at making the grand final.

Injury room

Kingsway emerged from the match with a clean bill of health

Willetton suffered the loss of star forward Brad with a hamstring tear, but otherwise they emerged unscathed.
